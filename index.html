Operations research I

Operational research (OR) encompasses a wide range of problem-solving techniques and methods applied in the pursuit of improved decision-making and efficiency, such as simulation, mathematical optimization, queueing theory and other stochastic-process models, Markov decision processes, econometric methods, data envelopment analysis, neural networks, expert systems, decision analysis, and the analytic hierarchy process. Nearly all of these techniques involve the construction of mathematical models that attempt to describe the system. Because of the computational and statistical nature of most of these fields, OR also has strong ties to computer science and analytics. Operational researchers faced with a new problem must determine which of these techniques are most appropriate given the nature of the system, the goals for improvement, and constraints on time and computing power.

WATER FILTER FOR CAMPERS

Fig. Design of the first prototype

The problem: Pollution of rivers and lakes in Peru means a great risk for national and foreign campers who visit different parts of the country looking for outdoor activities. For this reason, campers are forced to carry their own water to do their personal hygiene. Therefore, this limits their stay.

The goal: Develop a water filter that is comfortable, portable, easy to use and to do a quick maintenance, allowing campers to use it to reduce pollution of the rivers and use the water for personal hygiene. This will also help to decrease the risk of getting skin diseases. At the same time, to be able to recognize some minerals that are used for water purification.

Methodology: For the elaboration of the water filter, the materials to be used will be: cotton, stones, sand and activated carbon with a height of 25 cm and a width of 10 cm. With the intention of diminishing the water pollution of the rivers that the campers will use for its personal hygiene.

Acknowledgements: We thank UTEC for supporting this project and Prof. Juan Carlos Rodríguez for his advice.

OPTIMIZATION OF WATER FILTER FOR CAMPERS

Fig. Prototype in Solidworks and with 3D printing

The goal: Print the prototype of the water filter and evaluate the materials to be placed in the filter.

Methodology: For the manufacture of functional prototype divide the project into two parts: the design of the filter parts and materials that make up the cartridge. The next step was to materialize the concept in which we use "Dimension Elite" 3D printing, manufacturing of the components of the filter time lasted 48 hours. In order to remove any particles or toxic substances, we have chosen undergo all the parts in a washing process that lasted 24 hours. For the selection of the materials that make up the process of filtering we use the results of an investigation carried out by the group of "Applied to water disinfection nanotechnology" formed by Bryan Alcazar, Mijail Salcedo and Boris Estrada. Their analysis suggested the use of silver nanoparticles which can stick in the activated carbon.

Acknowledgements: We thank UTEC for supporting this project, Prof. Juan Carlos Rodríguez for his advice.Also to Bryan Alcázar Romani y Mijail Boris Salcedo Armas for their collaboration in the process of 3D printing.

Analysis of adsorbent materials

Fig. Materials adsorbents and bacteriological analysis.

The goal: Select the best materials to incorporate them in the filter cartridge.

Methodology: An analysis was realized with three adsorbent materials: diatomite, sand and activated carbon. Which went through a washing process in domestic acid and hydrogen peroxide. Also silver nanoparticles were impregnated to these three materials to observe the efficacy of each one. Later, series of analysis was realized as pH, conductivity, turbidity and bacteriological analysis

Acknowledgements: We thank UTEC for supporting this project, Prof. Juan Carlos Rodríguez for his advice.
